RRK Framework

Recursive–Relational Knowledge · A multi‑level dynamical theory

What is the RRK Framework?

The RRK Framework is a multi‑level theory of dynamical systems based on recursive–relational structure. It describes how systems evolve locally, how attractors form structured families, and how entire attractor landscapes reorganize under parameter changes.

Formally, an RRK system is represented as:

\[ \mathcal{R} = (X, F, R, \Theta, \Lambda), \]

where \(X\) is the state space, \(F\) the update rule, \(R\) the relational structure, \(\Theta\) operational parameters, and \(\Lambda\) the parameter space for global transitions.

What is RRK?

The Recursive‑Relational Framework (RRK) is a unified theory of system dynamics. It describes how systems evolve through the interplay of recursive feedback and relational coupling. RRK provides a conceptual, numerical, analytical, and global perspective on systemic change.

RRK Versions

  • RRK 2.0 – Conceptual and structural foundation
  • RRK 2.1 – Numerical dynamics and simulation
  • RRK 2.2 – Analytical stability theory
  • RRK 3.0 – Global attractor geometry

Research Program

The RRK research program develops a multi‑layered understanding of complex systems:

  • Conceptual mechanisms (RRK 2.0)
  • Simulation‑based exploration (RRK 2.1)
  • Analytical structure (RRK 2.2)
  • Global dynamics and transitions (RRK 3.0)

Together, these layers form a coherent scientific framework for studying systemic change.

Papers & Preprints

The RRK framework is documented in a series of scientific papers. New versions and extensions will be added as the research program evolves.

What is RRK?

RRK is a formal multi‑level framework for universal system dynamics. It explains how systems stabilize, destabilize, transition, and reorganize across scales.

The framework consists of four complementary layers:

  • RRK 2.0 – Conceptual Foundation
  • RRK 2.1 – Numerical Dynamics Model
  • RRK 2.2 – Analytical Stability Theory
  • RRK 3.0 – Global Dynamics & Transformation Theory

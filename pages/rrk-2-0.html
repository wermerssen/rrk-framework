RRK 2.0 – Local Dynamics

Recursive–relational systems and local attractor structure

Overview

RRK 2.0 formalizes the recursive–relational framework at the level of local dynamics. It focuses on how systems evolve in state space under fixed parameters, and how local attractors and their neighborhoods are organized.

At its core, RRK 2.0 studies a dynamical system \( F : X \to X \) on a state space \(X\), together with a recursive–relational structure that constrains how states can influence each other over time.

Recursive–relational structure

The recursive–relational aspect of RRK 2.0 is captured by a relation \( R \subseteq X \times X \) that encodes which states are relationally connected. The dynamics are then studied not only in terms of trajectories, but in terms of how they respect or violate this relational structure.

A simple formalization is:

\[ (x, y) \in R \quad \Rightarrow \quad F(x) \sim F(y), \]

where \(\sim\) denotes a notion of dynamical similarity

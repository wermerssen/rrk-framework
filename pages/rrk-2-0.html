RRK 2.0 – Conceptual Foundation

Recursive and relational structure of systems

Overview

RRK 2.0 establishes the conceptual core of the Recursive‑Relational Framework. It defines how systems evolve through the interplay of recursive feedback loops and relational coupling between components.

Recursive Mechanisms

Recursion describes how a system’s current state influences its next state. RRK 2.0 formalizes this as the fundamental driver of systemic evolution.

Relational Coupling

Components of a system do not evolve independently. Their interactions form relational structures that shape the system’s behavior and stability.

From Concept to Dynamics

RRK 2.0 provides the conceptual foundation for the numerical exploration in RRK 2.1 and the analytical structure in RRK 2.2. Together, these layers prepare the transition to the global theory in RRK 3.0.

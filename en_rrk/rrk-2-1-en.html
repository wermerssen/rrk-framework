RRK 2.1 – Drift & Stabilization

How recursive systems change without falling apart

Abstract

RRK 2.1 describes how recursive systems remain stable despite constant change. Drift arises from minimal deviations in recursion, while stabilization emerges from internal corrective mechanisms.

1. Drift

Drift refers to the systematic shift of the system state through repeated recursion:

\[ S_{t+1} = R(S_t) + \epsilon_t \]

Small errors \( \epsilon_t \) accumulate and produce long‑term change.

2. Stabilization

Systems stabilize themselves through internal counterforces:

\[ S_{t+1} = R(S_t) - K(S_t - S^\*) \]

Here, \( S^\* \) is an attractor or target state.

3. Drift–Stability Balance

Systems remain functional when drift and stabilization are in balance.

RRK 3.0 – Transformations

How systems transition into one another

Abstract

RRK 3.0 extends recursion by introducing transformations between systems. Systems can be mapped into one another without losing their internal logic.

1. Transformation

A transformation is a mapping:

\[ T: \mathcal{S}_A \rightarrow \mathcal{S}_B \]

2. Recursive Transformation

Transformations themselves can be recursive:

\[ S_{t+1} = T(R(S_t)) \]

3. Transition to RRK 4.0

RRK 4.0 investigates meta‑dynamics across many transformations.

RRK 2.2 – Attractors & Divergence

Why systems remain stable — or drift apart

Abstract

RRK 2.2 describes the long‑term patterns of recursive systems: attractors, cycles, chaos, and divergence.

1. Attractors

An attractor is a state or region toward which the system tends:

\[ R(S) \to S^\* \]

2. Divergence

Divergence occurs when small deviations are amplified:

\[ |R'(S)| > 1 \]

3. Chaotic Regions

Some systems possess no stable attractors but instead exhibit sensitivity‑dependent dynamics.
